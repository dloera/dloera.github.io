So learning this was quite interesting; it makes sense of the need for consolidating the different characters into one system, while solving the need for more characters, as all languages don't use the same characters. Web 2.0

I don't believe that I have seen this video before, but boy does it bring me back 10-15 years. Has the internet really changed that much? I was interested in the concept of Web 2.0, which is largely defined as the creation of websites where we share our information. Instead of creating a website that was only browsing, suddenly the idea of creating a platform to share those ideas became popular. This has led to the rise of things like Netflix, Facebook, Canvas, ... It is also largely predicated on the idea that we are selectively categorizing all of the content ourselves based on our searches, such as google. The second term I wanted to research from the video was machine learning, which are based on the algorithms created to take input from the external world, and allow the computer to search for the best solution to a problem. The reference in the video is largely based on Google (or at the time yahoo as well), of finding what the best served website for what a user is searching for.
